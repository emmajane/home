What should I expect?

For many reasons: a sense of self-worth, video conferences (what if you have to stand up?), answering the door, etc. Also, talk about independence and informality.
There are lots of distractions at home, these can range from human, to animal, to simple household chores.
Note that at most companies your setup at home will be entirely on you in terms of cost. While this might seem obvious and easy, consider that even a simple, but decent office chair at a big company will be several hundred dollars.
This freedom comes in many forms, but requires the worker to have initiative, focus, and to some degree ambition.
Combat isolation by simply getting out of the house. This doesn't have to be during work hours - look into various user groups, happy hours, physical activities, etc.

Confession

How do I manage these people?

A lack of physical interaction requires more touchpoints, not fewer. Not just chatting, but consider code reviews, (virtual) pair programming, and reports from other team members.
Engage on a personal level and realize that without an office environment, everyone is that much more unique in their needs and desires.
Don't treat remote workers as outcasts!
Okay, maybe not WHENEVER you feel like it
but yes, working from home *can* mean you work when it makes sense for you.
